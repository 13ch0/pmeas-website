Who We Are

This project was developed by four Wayne State University students for the CSC4996 (Senior Project). Under the guidance of Dr. Khayyam Hashmi and Dr. Sam Bryfczynski, the project evolved from a simple application with a basic UI to a more fully featured application with an accompanying mobile application. For any questions about the project, you may contact the developers by e-mail or by filling out the Contact Us Form.

Zein Hijazi is a senior at Wayne State. He likes long walks on the beach and playing games in his free-time. For the project, he worked primarly on the development of the audio modulation component of the project as well as the Android application.

Lee Lazarecky is a senior at Wayne State. Lee enjoys writing emulation applications and shilling for Qt. For the project, he worked primarly on the development of the main frontend application utilizing Qt as the framework.

Joshua Walters is a senior at Wayne State. He enjoys playing instruments in his free-time. For the project, he worked primarily on supporting the frontend development and backend components of the system.

Matthew Gaertner is a senior at Wayne State. Matthew enjoys arguing with Lee about why C is superior over C++. For the project, he worked primarly on the development of the bridge component, splash screen loading sequences, and the Android core logic before the effort models.
